# General Facts
These are general facts about the game.

## Evo VII Rally’s Broken Gears
This is more of a bug, but when you select the “Evo VII Rally” car from the menu and see it’s specs, you see that it has a “5 Speed” gearbox, which makes sense, but selecting the car in Manual and going in a race, you see that you can shift up to a 6th gear, but the 6th gear is totally unusable as it does not do anything, no production of power, and acts more like a neutral.

This works as early as the demo builds of the game and seems to work in all regions of the game.

## JGTC ESSO SUPRA Livery Change
In the US version of the game, the ESSO Supra race car is actually an EXXON Superflo Supra, while in the JP version, the car is the ESSO Ultraflo Supra.

## Removed Cars And Brands
In several versions of the game, most importantly, the Official Playstation Magazine US and Playstation 2 Magazine demo release and Japanese trial release of the game, there are mentions of several cars in the game string files, but does not actually appear in the final game. 

They seems to be a leftover text of the car that was supposed to appear, but was cut from development. 

From the OPM Demo of Enthusia, some more removed cars were found
- `フェラーリ Ｆ４０` = `Ferrari F40`
- `フェラーリ ３６０モデナ` = `Ferrari 360 modena`
- `ベントレー コンチネンタルＧＴ` = `BENTLEY Continental GT`
- `BUGATTI ＥＢ１１０ GT` = `BUGATTI EB110 GT` (technically is there as a Dauer)

Also in terms of brand
- `アキュラ` = `Acura`
- `いすゞ` = `ISUZU`
- `フェラーリ` = `Ferrari`
- `ブガッティ` = `Bugatti` (basically Dauer in final game)
- `ベントレー` = `Bentley`
- `マクラーレン` = `Mclaren` (we believe they mean the `BMW Mclaren F1 Race Car`, which in final game is referred by `BMW`)
- `マセラッティ` = `Maserati` (probably depreciated faster than the game died)
- `ポルシェ` = `Porsche`

For example, there also seems to be an unused “W” Engine in the game that has not been used, which would be perfect for a `Bentley`

A retail copy car list is available at [car_detail.md document](car_detail.md)

## TCS + ESC Screen
In the Japanese version and PAL version of the game, there is always a screen that pops up for TCS and ESC settings as soon as you start the game, which is not at all present in the US Release of the game.

![tcs-esc-screen](../assets/images/facts-and-glitches/tcs-esc-screen.png)

## CAR IS STUCK text
In the map “MIRAGE CROSSING”, there is a special text that says “CAR IS STUCK”. But from the game’s string files and code, there seems to be another text saying “The car is now off the track. Restarting.". This text does not trigger anywhere naturally, but can be triggered by changing some assembly while debugging. This was probably part of a Mirage Time Attack mode, which is not in the final game.

![car-stuck](../assets/images/facts-and-glitches/car-stuck.png)

In the OPM build of the game, the string of "CAR IS STUCK" was actually "The car is now stuck in the sand. Restarting."

**CAR STUCK, CAR STUCK, PLEASE, I BEG YOU.**

## Game Builds Dates
As per the game files, and many other sources including a leaked prototype build of the game, the JP build of the game (not the release, the actual compiled code) is 8 days OLDER than the US build of the game, despite the JP build release before the US build

## Unused Maps
Cosmic Winding Reverse and Wild West Enduro Reverse are two maps that exist in the game but are unused. Wild West Enduro Reverse does not have vertex or shading data, so it is a driveable black void.

There are also maps like Garage and Pylon Slalom and Demo Run and SS Circle that are not used, similar to wild west enduro reverse but this time without any driveable data.

You can read about it more at [map_detail.md](map_detail.md)

## Ranking Points
Ranking Points are basically allow you to progress in the game. That means that knowing which rank is given at how many points can help in precise calculations. You can read more about it at [ranking_points.md document](ranking_points.md)

## Drag Race
Ocean bridge is a track that can be used as a Drag Racing track in the game in Time Attack, but becomes endless when driving in Test Drive mode.

## Ocean Bridge Illusion
We used to think that Ocean bridge is infinite, but it is infact not infinite. It is all an illusion.

When you start on ocean bridge, behind you are barriers. Using debug car, if you get past them, you can see that the map does end there.

Also when you go and drive normally in the map, at around 3000m away from start, the game silently teleports the player back to 2000m in an infinite loop, and it is so seamless that it seems like infinite.

## Different VGS Versions
In the preview and old demos of Enthusia (like the E3 2004 Build), the VGS icon display is different from the release version, only showing the car's lateral and longitudinal G-forces and car's real direction of travel relative to the player's view instead of the weight transfer and tyre grip display we have today.

There is also screenshots of another variation of VGS icon.

Apparently, there were total of three versions of VGS -
- `"Ball Type"`
- `"Deluxe Type"`
- `"Arrow Type"`

## Interesting AI Behavior
There are several places where the AI seems to slow down more than they should, giving you better chances of winning in slower cars:
- The chicane in Marco Strada.
- The turn that connects the tarmac and dirt roads in Autumn Hill / Mountain.
- The two chicanes in Pacific Gateway.
- The massive banked turn in Löwenseering.

## Out Of Bounds Experience
Even though Enthusia’s collision system is amazing at not allowing you to clip outside the map, if you do manage to get out of the map somehow, especially in tracks like Ocean Bridge and Airport Square, and drive to the end of the map, some weird things tend to happen.

## Dirt / Snow on Cars
Cars tend to get dirt and snow on them when on rally tracks. Very small detail, but a neat one. Even Gran Turismo 4 does not have this detail.

## Black Flag behaviour
In the US build of the game, the Black Flag on the right side disappears as soon as you get it. But in JP and EU version of the game, it stays for the rest of the lap in less opacity.

## CD Projekt Distribution
In Poland, Konami released Enthusia, but it was not distributed by Konami. Instead, it was published, BY CD PROJEKT?

## ENTHU POINTS
In the Japan version of the game, ENTHU POINTS are not actually called ENTHU POINTS, they are instead called DRIVER HP, which makes much more sense than ENTHU POINTS in the US and PAL release of the game.

Also, it was previously just referred to as Driver HP in older versions too.

## Active Aero
This game features active aero for some cars. For example
- Dauer EB110 features an opening wing as soon as you join a race.
- Mercedes Benz Mclaren SLR has active wing. As soon as you go above 95kmph / 59mph, the wing opens up and keeps on. When you brake above that speed threshold, the wing does an airbrake till the threshold and afterwords, completely retreats.

![airbrake](../assets/images/facts-and-glitches/airbrake.png)

## Rotary Engine Chime
When you hit the RPM limit on a rotary car like the RX7 or the RX8, you hear a subtle engine chime that is meant to indicate that you have hit the max rpm and you should now shift up

## First Gen Prius Pedestrian Warning
If you drive the First Generation Prius in low speed, under a certain threshold like 45mph, you hear a pedastrian warning chime, its really subtle.

## Advanced AI
The AI in this game is truly absurd. They can seemingly adapt to any car changes you throw at them, even if these changes are not intended. For example, disabling ABS using custom cheat instructions. The AI learns that it will not be able to full brake, so it half brakes to avoid locking up the wheels. Another example is mass and physics changes. Changing those also makes the AI adapt.

## Suspension Travel
This game simulates suspension travel very accurately. SUVs and such have very high suspension travel, which functions very accurately. Similarly, sedans have less travel. This is most visible with jumps in maps like Mirage Crossing and Pacific.

Also, increasing or decreasing mass of cars using memory editing results in suspension height changing.

## Speedometer Reading
The speedometer reading in this game works weirdly. Firstly, it is not vehicle speed, but is the wheel speed instead. Secondly, the speedometer reading is delayed to be one update every 20 frames, or 3 updates per second, which is meant to show the digital speedometer delay of that time. This delay can be removed by using the "Remove Speedometer Delay" in PCSX2.

## Car Paints
The car paints in this game arent textures like GT4, but are actual paints.

The car's base paint is always white, and then layers of colors (upto 10 color layers) can be added on top to make different colors.

The colors are not limited to just what you see. Using memory editing, you can change the color to actual RGBA value, and create multiple color layers, which can create perlescent colors for cars like TVR.

![green-miot](../assets/images/facts-and-glitches/green-miot.png)
![tvr-changed](../assets/images/facts-and-glitches/tvr-changed.png)

Please ignore the "snowy pacific gateway map" and the "HDR TVR" and an uninvited guests watching the event.

## Mirage’s Track Randomization
Mirage is a very unique map, as in, it has a lot of track randomization. Most of it is very minor, and barely noticeable but some of it is quite obvious.

There are a total of 16 variations that can appear.

The game rolls the RNG every week to choose one of the 16 variation of mirage.

## Engine Simulation
This game simulates engine to a very extreme degree. It doesn't simulate moving parts, but it simulates the engine as a unit with its own drag, friction, etc.

## Driver Weight
Every car's weight in race is increased by 70 KG. That means that the driver's weight is being added to the car, and the driver canonically weighs 70KG.

## Japanese Text in US Build
You can enable Japanese text for cars and maps in the game by using memory editing. This also results in changing of the units from imperial to metric.

![jp-txt](../assets/images/facts-and-glitches/jp-txt.png)

## Hybrid Car Cruise Assist
The Prius and Estima, when you let go of the throttle, will use the electric power of the car to keep on cruising, so it slows down less. This is visible using the VGS where you can see torque applied to the car.

The Insight also holds speed but under 8kmph or 4mph. This is probably used for Stop-And-Go traffic in real life. Even if you hold the clutch on the Insight, it will keep on cruising.

- In aspiration, they called `Turbocharged` `TB` in short form, which, uh, could be misinterpreted as `Tuberculosis`. No wonder they changed it in final game. Call it `Turboculosis`.

## Debug Car
In the game’s code, there seems to be a random debug mode for the cars that you can enable by memory editing. It seems that this was a leftover from the development environment of the game and developers used it to quickly complete races.

## VGS Patent
VGS as a technology was so great, that the technology was literally patented in multiple countries, with the patent number `2004134629 (JP)` / `20070209436A1 (US)`, which you can search on public patent. This patent in the US is now abandoned. 

However, strangely enough, the Japanese patent seems to still have its renewal payments fulfilled by Konami up to as late as the 13th of January 2023, though it is now considered expired and its IP rights for Konami have been lost.

# Map Facts
## Rev City
- When you stop before turn 1 inside the turn in forward layout, and look back / turn back, you can see pedastrians walking in the walkway above in the tunnel with the shadows showing.
- The cameras in Rev City tunnel blink a red light.
- There are trucks driving around in a road on the track.
- You can see planes flying from Aiport Square into Rev City.

## Route de La Seine
- On the left side of the map using debug car, you can see a track map that is different from the final map. Apparently that map is closer to the real life roads there, but due to having an awkward sharp turn layout, they had to change it so it becomes easier and more fun for players to drive.

## Burgerschlucht
- This was previously called "Werz Bahn" in the game files.

## Nurburgring Nordschleife
- Nordschleife as a track is mostly height accurate, with the highest point in game coordinate system being accurate to the height from ocean level in real life (~620m)

## Airport Square
- You can see Rev City in the background.
